The largest consecutive-divisible subsequence

Given the integer \( 1 \le n \le 10^3 \) and the array \( A[1\ldots n] \) of natural numbers, not exceeding \( 2 \cdot 10^9 \). Output the maximum \( 1 \le k \le n \), for which there will be a subsequence \( 1 \le i_1 \lt i_2 \lt \ldots \lt i_k \le n \) with length \( k \), each element of which is divisible by the previous one (formally: for all \( 1 \le j \lt k \), \( A[i_j]\, | \,A[i_{j+1}] \)).


Sample Input:
4
3 6 7 12

Sample Output:
3


Memory limit: 256 Mb
Time limit: 5s
